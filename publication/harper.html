Title: "To Kill a Mockingbird"
Author: Harper Lee
Publication Date: 1960

Publication Details:
"To Kill a Mockingbird" is a renowned novel by Harper Lee, published in 1960. Set in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama, during the 1930s, the story revolves around Scout Finch, a young girl who narrates her experiences growing up in a racially divided society. The novel explores themes of racial injustice, morality, and the loss of innocence. Through the trial of a black man falsely accused of rape, the book examines issues of prejudice, compassion, and the search for justice. "To Kill a Mockingbird" has received critical acclaim for its compelling storytelling and powerful portrayal of social issues, making it a classic in American literature.
